Ray of Hope International Foundation,USA

"Ray of Hope International Foundation (www.letsgivehope.org) based in Philadelphia led by Anwesha Dutta & Hemang Desai has been supporting Gwalior Children's charity's programme in Gwalior, Central India, since 2012. Ray of Hope foundation in USA has been helping us for rural health care work since 2012 and part sponsored school bus, ambulance, water tank, solar installation. They donated the mobile van which transports children form neighboring villages to the school. They have helped enabling the scaling of the rural health program to 6 days a week, impacting nearly 150,000 lives a year. Additionally, Ray of Hope has contributed to many other projects like solar panel installation, ambulance, water storage tank capital project. Most recently Ray of Hope is supporting our boundary wall build project, which is an essential need for safety and security of the residents in our Snehalaya - The Home with Love in Gwalior. Ray of Hope continues to support the rural health program annually in addition to new projects and are honored to work with Dr. Sharma and the excellent team at Gwalior Children's Charity and Snehalaya."

Snehalaya is a multi-disciplinary campus based near Gwalior City, Madhya Pradesh, India. Spread across 25 acres, Snehalaya is a community living project that promotes environmental, economic and cultural sustainability for vulnerable, marginalised and underprivileged people. It has three areas of focus
Special Needs facility and orphanage where more than 60 disabled orphans or abandoned children and homeless women – most of whom have suffered neglect and abuse – live with carers inside cottages, play and learn

The education and empowerment of rural girls through Snehalaya School Rural health programme supporting poor, rural and marginalised communities

Snehalaya in www.thebiggive.org.uk


The school started in 2011 with 77 children mostly drop out girls from surrounding villages & slums. The girls are provided everything for free as well as incentive of Rs.10 per day for attending and completing the schooling. Nearly 300 children mostly, drop out girls are being educated.


In addition to Health Centre in campus providing health care services to the residents, students and poor from local community, Snehalaya runs mobile clinics working for integrated development in surrounding villages and slums including hygiene, sanitation, vaccination, safe drinking water, nutrition, maternal & child care, health education, awareness & medical treatment on their door steps as well as advice for self employment and income generation etc.

We welcome volunteers from all walks of life, skilled or unskilled, including trainees and students in any discipline for any duration to help and work with us either on floor in Snehalaya at Gwalior (India) or from their own place as per their convenience
