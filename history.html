History Of Snehalaya

1997

Gwalior Hospital Education & Charitable trust was Founded in 1997 by Dr Bal Krishna Sharma, Paediatric Surgeon in the NHS,UK. By taking early retirement Dr.Sharma spend his full time for the needs and smooth running of the Trust. The trust is run by board consisting of renowned professionals from India and abroad as board members..

1998-2004


In 1998, The trust started it's initial work by setting up Gwalior Childrens Hospital with mobile clinics in villages and slums. Later, it was expanded into Gwalior Children's & Women's hospital with eye centre and then into a General hospital with a mobile hospital(Van that provide clinical care through out villages and slums)on wheels. By the year 2004 we started providing medical care to poor and needy of Gwalior and Chambal region especially those in slums, villages, schools and orphanages..

2004-2010

"Snehalaya - The Home with Love", The Care Home was founded in 2004 in Gwalior, India. Since 2000 while providing medical care to the children in Orphanages including Mercy Home,Dr Bal Krishna Sharma happened to see the pathetic conditions of children with disabilities living in Mercy Home. And those abandoned in streets, Snehalaya was established, where such children could be provided all care with love and dignity living in family environment. With training and better care, these children could become independent as much possible and lead a life with self esteem and dignity living in this care home named "Snehalaya - The Home with Love".

The care home started with five cottages and 40 children in 2006 and expanded gradually adding a health centre in 2007, five more cottages in 2008, Gaushala in 2009. Snehalaya mostly has disabled, abandoned, homeless residents, who otherwise would have been forced to live on streets with nobody else to support them for food, clothes, shelter and other daily needs including education, medical care and training. There are not any government or privately run organisation in Gwalior, India, where they can receive proper education, training, and care. Established in 2006, as a care home for the disabled, destitute, children and homeless women, where they live together as a family with love and dignity in ten cottages with their carer parents. At present, there are 65 residents and it is planned to accommodate 200 in the near future when completed. "Snehalaya meaning The Home with Love in Hindi" is the name given to this facility, perhaps only one of its type in whole India. It runs a special needs school with multi sensory room and vocational training facilities for differently-abled in Gwalior, India.

2011-2017

In 2011, the Trust started a mainstream school within Snehalaya Campus, to educate poor children especially drop out girls from villages to get them out of a life of oppression, which these girls are fated otherwise. At present nearly 350 children, mostly drop out girls gets education from the school. The eligible girls are provided everything for free as well Rs. 10 per day to inspire and motivate them to complete their schooling. This helps with women empowerment giving them an opportunity to become independent to improve the standard of life not only for them but also their families and future generations. We aim to educate 1000 poor children every year in our mainstream school.

2018

First batch of girls have just completed their schooling and passed exams of 12th standard (equivalent A level exams or Jr. college in UK) in 2018. We are happy that all of them are motivated to go for higher education including professional courses. The trust will support them for their higher education.

In addition through our mobile clinics with base at health centre located within Snehalaya campus, we also help near by villages with health education and awareness including hygiene, sanitation, vaccination, nutrition for child and mother, medical care, safe drinking water, income generation etc. For lack of electricity in villages, we also help school going children in villages and their family by supplying solar lanterns to facilitate their education and to prevent pollution helping environment in villages of Gwalior, India. We give them a better life through our school & care home in Gwalior, India, which is one of the least developed and oppressed areas of central India.

We started a mainstream school to educate them, to empower them, so that they can lead the life of their own choice and make it better not only for themselves, but also for their whole family and future generations.
