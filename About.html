Who we are

We are the trust named (Gwalior Hospital Education & Charitable trust)established with the aim to Help Children Of India in the village Gwalior,India.

The trust is Founded by Dr Bal Krishna Sharma retired paediatric Surgeon in the NHS,UK.

Having seen the abandoned children on streets DR Bal Krishna Sharma started the trust to provide food & home for the needy in Gwalior,India.

The care home mostly has disabled, abandoned, homeless residents who otherwise would have been forced to live on streets with nobody else to support for food and shelter.

There are not many government or privately run organisation in Gwalior where they can receive proper education, training, and care.

Established in 2004, as a care home for the disabled, destitute, children and homeless women, where they live together as a family with love and dignity. At present, there are 65 residents and we hope to to accommodate 200 in the near future."Snehalaya" is the name given the school and care home.

We give them a better life through our school & care home in Gwalior,India which is one of the least developed areas of central India

We started a mainstream school to educate them, to empower them, so that they can lead the life of their own choice and make it better not only for themselves, but also for their whole family and future generations.

Why did we build our Trust in India -

In India especially in the state of Madhya Pradesh, almost 2.5 % of the total number of children are differently-abled. Because of illiteracy, myths and lack of awareness, these children are mostly neglected and abused. Most of such children are kept isolated in homes and are constantly looked down upon. They do not get an equal opportunity to education and vocational training. Many of them are abandoned on streets even by their parents, left to fend for themselves or on the mercy of society or government. There are not many government or privately run places where they can receive proper education, training, and care. Having been through a similar experience, and having seen the conditions of such children in government run mercy homes in Gwalior, we established our Snehalaya in Central India, to care for, to feed, to educate, and to train such children so that they can live a life with love and dignity and become independent as much possible."
